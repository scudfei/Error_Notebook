*   **‚úÖ Correct Option:** Frodo‚Äôs discomfort with how easily the truth can be dismantled by the "shrewd" observation of a practical mind.

### üí° In-Depth Analysis
*   **Context:** Maggot is speculating about the Riders. He guesses "gold and jewels". This is technically wrong (it's the Ring), but *thematically* close (treasure/secret).
*   **Evidence:** "Frodo said nothing: the shrewd guesses of the farmer were rather disconcerting. ... 'Maybe you're right,' said Frodo, avoiding the farmer's eye..."
*   **Reasoning:** Frodo isn't angry or distrustful in a malicious way. He is **disconcerted** (unsettled) because a simple farmer saw through the secrecy to the core issue (Bilbo's "treasure"). Avoiding eye contact is a defense mechanism against being "read" further.
*   **Distractor Trap:** **Plausible Misreading** - Interpreting discomfort as "irritation" or "arrogance". Frodo respects Maggot's shrewdness, which is exactly why he fears it.

## ‚ùå Question 9: The River-Mist

**Question:** The "river-mist" that begins to rise as the hobbits approach the Ferry serves to...
*   **‚úÖ Correct Option:** Create an atmosphere of literal and metaphorical uncertainty as the hobbits approach a major threshold.

### üí° In-Depth Analysis
*   **Context:** They are nearing the Bucklebury Ferry, the border of their familiar world.
*   **Evidence:** "Thin strands of river-mist were hanging above the dikes, and crawling over the fields. 'It's going to be thick,' said Maggot..."
*   **Reasoning:** In literature, mist often symbolizes **uncertainty**, **transition**, or the **unknown**. Here, it rises as they leave the Shire (safety) for the wild (danger). It physically obscures vision, mirroring their lack of clarity about the future.
*   **Distractor Trap:** **Lore-Trap** (Old Forest) or **Over-Interpretation** (Ring's power). The mist is primarily an atmospheric device to build tension, not necessarily a magical attack or a specific geography reference yet.
