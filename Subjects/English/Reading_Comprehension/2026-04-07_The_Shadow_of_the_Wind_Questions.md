## ❌ Question 1: Youth and age

**Question:** Which quotation from Barceló **best** illustrates the contrast between the attitudes of youth and age?
* **My Selection (B):** “and a cinnamon milk shake for the young one”
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **“Don’t add on any years, you rascal.”**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** Daniel wants to seem older; Barceló jokes that age comes quickly enough on its own.
* **Analysis:** Choice B shows an age difference, but not a contrast in **attitude toward age**. Choice A directly contrasts youthful eagerness to grow older with older awareness of passing time.
* **Conclusion:** The student noticed age, but not the more exact contrast in attitude.

## ❌ Question 2: “most saintly smile”

**Question:** Daniel offers Barceló his “most saintly smile.” This shows that Daniel is
* **My Selection (A):** respectful and naïve
* **✅ Correct Option (C):** **perceptive and cunning**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** Daniel realizes he must stay on good terms with Barceló to get information.
* **Evidence:** He deliberately performs delight rather than spontaneously feeling it.
* **Analysis:** The smile is strategic. The wording sounds innocent, which makes A tempting, but the surrounding narration shows calculation and social awareness.
* **Conclusion:** Literal surface reading led to the wrong answer.

## ❌ Question 3: Main purpose

**Question:** The author’s **main** purpose in this excerpt is to
* **My Selection (A):** create atmosphere
* **✅ Correct Option (B):** **develop character**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** The opening atmosphere is vivid, but most of the excerpt is devoted to Daniel, Barceló, and their interaction.
* **Analysis:** Atmosphere is present, but not primary. The passage mainly reveals personality, values, and power dynamics.
* **Conclusion:** This is a whole-passage logic issue: opening texture was over-weighted.

## ❌ Question 4: Barceló at the end

**Question:** Barceló’s distraction and withdrawal from debate at the end can **best** be described as
* **My Selection (B):** thoughtful
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **ominous**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Evidence:** “fake monocle” / “masked smile” / wolf-like interest.
* **Analysis:** The mood is not neutral reflection but concealed threat and predatory intent.
* **Conclusion:** Tone family was sensed, but not precisely named.

## ❌ Question 5: “specters”

**Question:** When using the word “specters” in the description of Els Quatre Gats, the narrator is referring to
* **My Selection (B):** honours
* **✅ Correct Option (C):** **ghosts**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** The café feels haunted by great figures from the past.
* **Analysis:** The diction invokes dead presences, not prestige in the abstract.
* **Conclusion:** A literary image was flattened into a more general prestige idea.

## ❌ Question 6: Barceló’s trait with the waiter

**Question:** The word that **best** describes the trait Barceló exhibits when he gives his order to the waiter is
* **My Selection (A):** tactless
* **✅ Correct Option (B):** **haughty**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** His behaviour is not merely socially clumsy; it is deliberately superior, performative, and condescending.
* **Conclusion:** The student sensed negativity but missed the more exact trait word.

## ❌ Question 7: “What is it you want?”

**Question:** Barceló’s tone when he says, “Let’s see junior. *What* is it you want?” is **best** described as
* **My Selection (D):** sardonic
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **exasperated**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** Barceló has tried money and persuasion; Daniel still refuses.
* **Analysis:** The tone is irritated impatience rather than cutting mockery.
* **Conclusion:** Another near-miss in tone precision.

---

## 📚 Key Vocabulary & Expressions

1. **ominous**: suggesting future danger or threat.
2. **haughty**: arrogantly superior.
3. **sardonic**: mocking in a bitter or scornful way.
4. **exasperated**: annoyed and impatient.
5. **characterization**: the way a text reveals character.
