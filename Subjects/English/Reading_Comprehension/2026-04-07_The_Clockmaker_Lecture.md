## ❌ Question 1: “lecture” on soft sawder

**Question:** In the phrase “his lecture on ‘soft sawder’ was cut short,” the word “lecture” is an example of
* **My Selection (A):** irony
* **✅ Correct Option (C):** **hyperbole**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** Mr. Slick is simply talking to the narrator, not literally giving a formal lecture.
* **Evidence:** The passage refers to an ordinary explanation/conversation, but labels it a “lecture.”
* **Analysis:** The narrator exaggerates the scale and authority of what Slick is saying. That makes **hyperbole** the best label. Irony is tempting because the usage is somewhat playful, but the main effect comes from overstatement.
* **Conclusion:** The student noticed the mismatch between word and situation, but chose the wrong rhetorical label.

---

## 📚 Key Vocabulary & Expressions

1. **hyperbole**: deliberate exaggeration for effect.
2. **irony**: a contrast between appearance and reality, often with implied meaning.
3. **soft sawder**: flattering talk; smooth persuasion.
4. **pedlar**: travelling seller.
