## ❌ Question 1: Best place to begin a paragraph

**Question:** One good place to begin a paragraph, according to Safire, is with
* **My Selection (C):** conjunctions like “but” or “however”
* **✅ Correct Option (B):** **numbers like “first” or “secondly”**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** C is genuinely mentioned later as a valid break point, which makes it tempting. But the question asks for the place Safire explicitly recommends in that specific statement: “an enumeration is always a good place.”
* **Conclusion:** This is a textbook **best-answer ranking** error.

## ❌ Question 2: “logomasochism”

**Question:** From the context of the sentence, the main meaning of the term “logomasochism” is to
* **My Selection (B):** love reading texts with few paragraph breaks
* **✅ Correct Option (C):** **inflict punishment on oneself through words**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** B stays tied to the essay topic, but C is more precise because the coined word literally combines the idea of words (*logo-*) with self-punishment (*masochism*).
* **Conclusion:** The student chose the topic-related option instead of the more exact meaning.

## ❌ Question 3: “redundancy”

**Question:** The “redundancy” of which Safire writes can best be described as referring to
* **My Selection (A):** his repetition of the phrase “shrug the shoulders”
* **✅ Correct Option (B):** **shrugging, which can only be about shoulders**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** The point is not that the phrase is repeated in the essay, but that **the noun is unnecessary because the verb already contains the meaning**.
* **Conclusion:** This is a grammar-and-reference precision problem.

## 📚 Key Vocabulary & Expressions

1. **enumeration**: listing items one by one.
2. **redundancy**: unnecessary repetition.
3. **context vocabulary**: meaning determined by surrounding use.
4. **neologism**: a newly invented word.
