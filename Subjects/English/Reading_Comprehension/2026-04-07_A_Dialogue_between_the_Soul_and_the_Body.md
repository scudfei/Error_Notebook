## ❌ Question 1: “physic yet could never reach”

**Question:** The Body’s accusation that “physic yet could never reach / The maladies thou me dost teach” means that
* **My Selection (C):** a Soul can never understand the physical side of existence
* **✅ Correct Option (D):** **no medicine can cure the illnesses that the Soul causes**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** “Physic” means medicine. The line says the Body suffers afflictions caused by the Soul that medicine cannot cure.
* **Conclusion:** The student drifted into a broader philosophical interpretation and missed the direct meaning.

---

## ❌ Question 2: Scansion

**Question:** Which example illustrates the correct scansion of the line?
* **My Selection (B)**
* **✅ Correct Option (A)**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** The poem is in **iambic tetrameter**: four iambic feet (unstressed + stressed). The correct scansion must match that pattern.
* **Conclusion:** This is a technical poetry-form weakness.

---

## ❌ Question 3: Unification of Body and Soul

**Question:** In order to convey the idea of unification of Body and Soul as a whole, the poet uses
* **My Selection (1):** Parallelism
* **✅ Correct Option (4):** **Irony**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** The irony is that Body and Soul complain against one another even though they are inseparably bound. Parallel structure exists, but irony best captures the larger conceptual effect.
* **Conclusion:** The student noticed form, but missed the governing interpretive device.

---

## ❌ Question 4: The Soul’s complaint

**Question:** The Soul’s complaints in the first stanza indicate that he resents being
* **My Selection (D):** bound by chains
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **trapped within the Body**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** Chains are the metaphorical image; being trapped in the Body is the larger meaning.
* **Conclusion:** The student selected the image instead of the interpretive idea.

---

## 📚 Key Vocabulary & Expressions

1. **physic**: medicine.
2. **malady**: illness or affliction.
3. **iambic tetrameter**: four iambic feet per line.
4. **parallelism**: similar grammatical structure.
5. **irony**: tension between what seems to be said and what is really revealed.
