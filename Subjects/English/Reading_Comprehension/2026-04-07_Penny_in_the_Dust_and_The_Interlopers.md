## ❌ Question 1: Penny in the Dust – “old sun-still fields”

**Question:** The meaning of “old sun-still fields” from the opening line could best be defined as
* **My Selection (D):** the place where they walked while reminiscing
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **bathed in sunlight**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** D describes the narrative situation, but not the phrase itself. The phrase is descriptive and atmospheric.
* **Conclusion:** The student paraphrased context instead of defining the expression.

---

## ❌ Question 2: Penny in the Dust – father’s manner

**Question:** The main reason why the narrator has difficulty describing his father’s manner while in his son’s presence is because
* **My Selection (B):** he could not find the words to describe his father
* **✅ Correct Option (C):** **his father did not know how to relate to his young son**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Evidence:** “his sure-footed way in the fields forsook him the moment he came near the door of my child’s world.”
* **Analysis:** The passage explicitly explains relational awkwardness, not mere verbal difficulty.
* **Conclusion:** The student chose the narrator’s surface complaint instead of the deeper reason he gives.

## ❌ Question 3: The Interlopers – setting details

**Question:** The particular details of setting are **most likely** included to
* **My Selection (C):** foreshadow Ulrich’s offer of friendship
* **✅ Correct Option (B):** **intensify a charged and violent conflict**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** The storm, darkness, and agitation of the forest amplify hostility and violence. Friendship comes much later and is not the primary function of the opening setting.
* **Conclusion:** The student over-read later plot development back into the initial setting.

---

## ❌ Question 4: The Interlopers – why guarded?

**Question:** Why was the “narrow strip of precipitous woodland” “jealously guarded”?
* **My Selection (C):** because the court had challenged Ulrich’s right to own the land
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **because a neighbour constantly challenged Gradwitz’s land claim**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** The court had supported Ulrich’s family claim; the problem was the neighbouring family’s refusal to accept it.
* **Conclusion:** The student reversed the position of court and rival family.

---

## 📚 Key Vocabulary & Expressions

1. **sun-still**: still and quiet in sunlight.
2. **precipitous**: very steep.
3. **foreshadow**: hint at what will happen later.
4. **intensify**: make stronger.
5. **characterization**: revealing a character’s nature.
