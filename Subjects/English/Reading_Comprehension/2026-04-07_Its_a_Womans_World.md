## ❌ Question 1: “living by the lights...”

**Question:** The phrase “living by the lights / of the loaf left / by the cash register” is **best** understood to mean
* **My Selection (C):** a woman’s chores are endless
* **✅ Correct Option (A):** **a woman is measured by her oversights**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Context:** The poem says women “milestone / our lives / with oversights” and are “defined / by what we forget.”
* **Analysis:** Choice C is concrete and plausible, but too literal. The image is not merely about endless chores; it is about how women’s lives are socially measured and remembered through small domestic failures and omissions.
* **Conclusion:** This is a strong example of the student preferring the concrete reading over the more abstract symbolic one.

---

## ❌ Question 2: “our windows / moth our children...”

**Question:** The phrase “our windows / moth our children / to the flame / of hearth” is **best** understood to mean that
* **My Selection (3):** our children peek out the windows and star-gaze
* **✅ Correct Option (2):** **bright lights attract our children to what is warm**

### 💡 In-Depth Analysis
* **Analysis:** The comparison is to moths drawn to flame. The focus is not stargazing but attraction back toward the domestic sphere, warmth, and household life.
* **Conclusion:** The student followed one visible image (“windows” / “starry mystery”) instead of the controlling metaphor.

---

## 📚 Key Vocabulary & Expressions

1. **oversight**: a mistake caused by forgetting or failing to notice.
2. **hearth**: the home fire; symbol of domestic life.
3. **symbolism**: using images to represent larger ideas.
4. **abstraction**: expressing a broad idea rather than a concrete object.
5. **milestone**: a marker of progress or stages in life.
